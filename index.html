FAQ

What is a hackathon?

Hackathons are where your crazy ideas become reality. Build anythingâ€”there are no restrictions on what you can create. Dive in and make the most incredible thing you can alongside fellow creators. While some may see hackers as the crazy ones, we see genius.

What if I don't have a team or an idea?

No problem! Many hackers do not have a fully-formed team or hack concept before the event begins. We will be hosting team-forming activities after hacking begins to help you find the perfect match. If you're a first-time hacker, there will be many others looking for team members with whom you can pair.

What kind of stuff should I build? Is there a theme?

Hackpoly doesn't have a theme and you can build whatever you like. At Hackpoly 2014, projects ranged from a hacked car, to an electric cello, to an app that helps track your pantry. At Hackpoly 2015, some projects consisted of a multiplayer scavenger hunt to instant presentation generators.

How big should teams be? Can I hack alone?

We highly encourage you to build with a team. Teamwork is deeply rooted in the hacking ethos, and you'll have a better time building with your brightest peers. Teams should be at most four people.

Who can attend?

In order to attend Hackpoly, you must have a valid .edu email address and can't have graduated more than 6 months prior.

What should I bring?

All attendees are required to bring their valid student ID for admission. Laptops, sleeping bags, and toiletries are highly recommended.

How will projects be judged?

You will demo your project to a panel of judges, then they will make decisions based on a combination of novelty, utility, design, overall coolness, and technical complexity.

How much does it cost?

Hackpoly is free for all accepted hackers. We will provide your WiFi, meals, caffeine, swag, and workspace for the entire weekend.

Does Hackpoly offer travel reimbursement?

Despite our best efforts, we are unable to provide travel reimbursement to out-of-state hackers at this time. Rest-assured you will be taken care of when you arrive with a weekend of free meals, tons of swag, prizes and a comfortable environment for you and your peers.

What are the rules?

Essentially just build cool stuff and don't be a jerk. More info here (Code of Conduct).

What if I have another question?

Please feel free to reach out to us via Facebook, Twitter, or email at info@hackpoly.com.

What if I can't code or do anything technical?

Well then this is your time to learn! Or as a wise man once said, just do it.
